Chicago Taxi — EDA & Hypothesis Testing (Weather)

Exploratory analysis and hypothesis testing to understand taxi activity in Chicago and evaluate whether trip duration from the Loop to O’Hare changes on rainy Saturdays.

Context & goal

  • Identify leading taxi companies by trip volume (Nov 15–16, 2017)
  • Find the top 10 dropoff neighborhoods by average trips (Nov 2017)
  • Test whether average trip duration (Loop → O’Hare) changes on rainy Saturdays

Data

  • project_sql_result_01.csv: taxi company trip counts (Nov 15–16, 2017)
  • project_sql_result_04.csv: average trips by dropoff neighborhood (Nov 2017)
  • project_sql_result_07.csv: Loop → O’Hare trips with weather and duration

Approach

  • Data checks: verified types, missing values, and basic consistency
  • EDA: ranked + visualized companies and neighborhoods
  • Hypothesis testing: defined H0/H1, selected alpha, and compared rainy vs non-rainy Saturdays

Hypothesis

  • H0: The average trip duration (Loop → O’Hare) is the same on rainy Saturdays and non-rainy Saturdays.
  • H1: The average trip duration (Loop → O’Hare) is different on rainy Saturdays vs non-rainy Saturdays.
  • Alpha: 0.05

Key findings

  • Top taxi companies: Flash Cab led trip volume on Nov 15–16, 2017, followed by Taxi Affiliation Services and Medallion Leasing.
  • Top dropoff areas: The Loop and River North concentrated the highest average dropoffs in Nov 2017.
  • Hypothesis test: Rainy Saturdays had longer trips (mean ≈ 2427s vs 2000s). With p-value ≈ 6.74e-12 < 0.05, we reject H0.
